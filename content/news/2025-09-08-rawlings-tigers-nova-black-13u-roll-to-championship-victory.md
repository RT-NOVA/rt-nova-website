The Rawlings Tigers NOVA – Black 13U put together a dominant Sunday at South County Middle School, securing both their semifinal and championship wins in convincing fashion.

In the semifinals, the Tigers jumped ahead early and never looked back, cruising to a 10–1 victory over the Metro Senators 13U Central. The pitching staff combined to allow just one unearned run across seven innings, while the offense delivered timely hits and aggressive baserunning to take control of the game.

The momentum carried into the championship matchup against Stars Camarata 13U, where the Tigers’ bats stayed hot. The team piled up 14 hits and capitalized on early opportunities, building a sizable lead by the third inning on the way to a 12–3 win. Strong pitching and steady defense kept the game out of reach, while a relentless offensive approach sealed the tournament title.

With contributions up and down the lineup and solid performances on the mound, the Tigers showcased depth, balance, and teamwork on their way to the championship crown.
